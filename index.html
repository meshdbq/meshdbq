Start here

Get into Meshtastic without getting overwhelmed.

The easiest path is to join the local group first, confirm the right hardware, and use the recommended settings before chasing advanced setups.

  1. Join the Facebook group. Meet local operators, ask beginner questions, and see what is working nearby.
  2. Buy Meshtastic-compatible US 915 MHz hardware. Ask the group before buying so you do not accidentally choose the wrong region or device.
  3. Flash firmware and connect the app. Use the official flasher and the Android, iOS, or Web client.
  4. Test from a better location. Height matters. Windows, upper floors, rooftops, ridges, and outdoor spots usually perform better.

Before using ROUTER or REPEATER roles

Coordinate with the group first. ROUTER and REPEATER roles are for strategic, stationary infrastructure. Used casually, they can create packet collisions, waste airtime, and make messages less reliable.

FAQ

Common beginner questions.

Short answers first. Details can come later in the group.

Do I need a license?

For normal Meshtastic LoRa use, a license is not required in the United States. Licensed amateur radio use is optional and comes with different privileges and restrictions, including restrictions around encryption.

Does it work without cell service?

Yes. Your phone or computer connects to a Meshtastic radio, and the radios communicate over LoRa. Cell service, Wi-Fi, and internet are not required for local mesh messages.

How far can Meshtastic reach?

Range depends on terrain, elevation, antenna placement, frequency, settings, and obstructions. Around Dubuque, hills, trees, buildings, and the river valley all matter.

What device should I buy first?

This page does not recommend a single starter device because hardware changes often. Look for Meshtastic-compatible hardware for the US 915 MHz region, then ask the Facebook group before buying.

Can I put a node at my house or business?

Yes. A stable, elevated location can help the local mesh. Use CLIENT or CLIENT_BASE, keep intervals long, and coordinate with the group before trying ROUTER or REPEATER roles.

Can I use it during emergencies?

Meshtastic can be useful for preparedness, events, and local backup communication, but it is not a guaranteed emergency service and does not replace 911, weather radios, official alerts, or public safety systems.
